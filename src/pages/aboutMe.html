About Me

I am a software engineer who currently works at Brenkman and Company. There I work on a variety of custom software projects including mobile apps, desktop apps, and web apps. I am directly involved in project design and management, UI/UX design, Dev Ops, communicating with clients, and have taken the lead on several projects.

I enjoy working on various side projects with software development. I also enjoy tinkering with Linux systems and software. I have a passion for usable secure and privacy-focused software and spend a great deal of time researching optimal solutions for various tasks.

My wife and I are both graduates from Utah State University. I got a degree in Computer Science there. While there I also participated in the Free Software and Linux Club, Hack USU, as well as other coding competitions.

Ever since I was young I worked for my Dad doing construction, mostly pouring concrete. I also spent a lot of time working on the family ranch riding horses, fixing fence, and doing other kinds of manual labor. Honest hard work has been a part of life as long as I can remember.

I love developing software that makes people's lives easier, safer, and more free. Most of the software people use on a daily basis is invasive and counterproductive as most business models from large coorporations include mining user data and manipulating user behavior. I want to provide usable, secure alternatives that improve people's lives without sacrificing their freedom and privacy.

Through my personal research, projects, and career I endever to keep an open mind and learn all I can to become the best software engineer, UX designer, and project manager I can.
